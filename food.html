Famous Foods of Bihar

Litti Chokha

Roasted wheat balls filled with spiced gram flour, served with mashed vegetables like potato, tomato, and brinjal.

Thekua

A crispy, sweet snack made with wheat flour, jaggery, and coconut. Mostly enjoyed during Chhath Puja.

Malpua

Sweet fried pancakes made of flour, banana, and milk, soaked in sugar syrup. A Holi special dessert.

Sattu Paratha

Stuffed flatbread filled with roasted gram flour mixed with herbs and spices, served with curd or chutney.

Dal Pitha

Steamed rice flour dumplings stuffed with spicy lentil filling. A traditional Bihari healthy snack.

Malai Kheer

A creamy dessert made with rice, milk, cardamom, and garnished with dry fruits. Popular in Bihari households.

Khaja

A crispy, flaky dessert similar to baklava, made with layered dough soaked in sugar syrup.

Ghujiya

A spicy curry made from black gram or peas, enjoyed with roti or puffed rice.

Kadhi Bari

Soft gram flour dumplings cooked in tangy yogurt-based gravy, served with rice.

Boondi Ladoo

Sweet balls made of small fried gram flour droplets soaked in syrup and bound together.

Parwal Ki Mithai

A unique sweet where pointed gourd is stuffed with khoya and nuts, especially loved in Bihar.
