Madhubani Painting (Mithila Painting)

Madhubani painting, also known as Mithila painting, is a major art tradition of the Mithila region of Bihar. This painting is mainly done by women and uses natural colours. This art started in 1960 with the late Mahasundari Devi, a sadhvi of Madhubani district, who introduced colourful painting. Since then women started adopting this art and today this art is famous all over the world.

Madhubani Painting

Woman artist making a picture. It is the main art of Darbhanga, Purnia, Saharsa, Muzaffarpur, Madhubani and some areas of Nepal in Bihar. Initially, this art was in the form of Rangoli, but gradually it has come in a modern form on clothes, walls and paper. This domestic art started by the women of Mithila has also been adopted by men. Currently, the artists of Mithila painting have decorated the walls of Madhubani railway station with Mithila painting in about 10,000 sq/ft to further increase the respect of Madhubani and Mithila painting at the international level. This initiative of theirs was done free of cost i.e. in the form of Shramdaan. This wonderful art work done in the form of Shramdaan is being liked a lot by foreign tourists and tourists.

Speciality

One of the specialties of Madhubani paintings is that they are made on wooden pieces. The main colors used are harmony, red, green, blue, and black which give speciality to this art.

History

It is believed that these paintings were made by King Janak through female artists during the marriage of Ram-Sita. Women of many villages of Mithila region are skilled in this art. In its original form, these paintings were seen in the mud-plastered huts of the villages, but now it is widely made on cloth or paper canvas. With time, the life story of King Shailesh also started being depicted by the people of Paswan caste community in this genre of painting. People of this community also worship King Sailesh as their deity.

Speciality (Detailed)

In this painting, the family deity is also depicted. Pictures of Hindu gods and goddesses, natural scenes like the sun and the moon, religious plants like Tulsi and wedding scenes can be seen. Madhubani paintings are of two types - wall paintings and Aripan or Alpana.

Methods

Bright colours are used a lot. Like deep red, green, blue and black. Some light colours are also used to enhance the painting, like yellow, pink and lemon colour. You will be surprised to know that these colours are made from household items, like turmeric, banana leaves, peepal bark is used for red colour. And milk. Apart from wall paintings, Alpana is also very popular in Bihar. It is made in the living room or outside the door. Earlier it was made so that the crop yield in the fields is good but nowadays it is made in auspicious works at home. Matchsticks and bamboo pens are used to make the painting. Gum of acacia tree is added to make the colour stick.

Meaning Over Time

Over time, the meaning behind creating Madhubani paintings has changed, but the art has so much in it that it remains among the favourite choices of art connoisseurs even today.

Handmade Paper

To prepare handmade paper before painting, a solution of cow dung is prepared on the paper and acacia gum is added to it. The solution of cow dung is applied on the paper with a cotton cloth and it is kept in the sun for drying.

Type

Madhubani paintings are currently created by painters on walls, canvas and handmade paper.

Graffiti

In Madhubani wall painting, a mixture of clay (smooth) and cow dung is mixed with acacia gum and plastered on the walls. Due to the presence of a special chemical substance in cow dung, the wall gets a special shine. There is a tradition of making it at three special places in the house, such as - Puja Sthan, Kohbar Room (married people's room) and on the outer walls of the house on the occasion of marriage or any special festival. The gods and goddesses depicted in Madhubani painting are - Maa Durga, Kali, Sita-Ram, Radha-Krishna, Shiva-Parvati, Gauri-Ganesh and ten incarnations of Vishnu etc. Apart from these pictures, paintings of many natural and beautiful scenes are also made. Animals, birds, trees, flowers, leaves etc. are decorated with the symbol of Swastika.

Attractions and Achievements

  • It was decided to include Mithila painting in the world record in a grand event held at Madhubani Railway Station on 27 September 2019.
  • In the last few years, the largest Mithila art gallery has been built at Madhubani railway station by the railway craft group and local artists, due to which the beauty of this place has become a topic of discussion in the world, people from different cities of the world are coming here to see it!
  • There is also a museum related to the history of Madhubani painting in Japan.
  • In 2018-2019, a train in Samastipur division of Indian Railways was run with Madhubani painting on it, which was highly appreciated in the world. 50-60 artists of Darbhanga Madhubani together decorated it with beautiful paintings with their tireless hard work.
  • Some of the main artists among them are Khushboo Chaudhary, Preeti Jha, Anjali Jha, Amarjyoti, Ragini, Munki, Ayushi.
